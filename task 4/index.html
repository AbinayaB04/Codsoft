Tribute to Mother Teresa

Biography

Mother Teresa, born AnjezÃ« Gonxhe Bojaxhiu on August 26, 1910, in Skopje (now in North Macedonia), was a Roman Catholic nun and missionary. She devoted her life to helping the poor, the sick, and the dying around the world. She founded the Missionaries of Charity, a religious congregation that manages homes for people dying of HIV/AIDS, leprosy, and tuberculosis, as well as soup kitchens, dispensaries, mobile clinics, children's and family counseling programs, orphanages, and schools. Mother Teresa was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1979 and was canonized as Saint Teresa of Calcutta in 2016.

Admirable Qualities

  • Compassion: Mother Teresa dedicated her life to caring for the sick and poor.
  • Selflessness: She lived a life of poverty and humility, prioritizing the needs of others above her own.
  • Perseverance: Despite numerous challenges, she persisted in her mission to help the most vulnerable.
  • Humility: She remained humble throughout her life, despite receiving global recognition and numerous awards.
  • Faith: Her deep faith in God was the driving force behind her tireless work and commitment to her mission.
  • Empathy: She had an extraordinary ability to empathize with those who were suffering, offering comfort and solace.
  • Leadership: She demonstrated exceptional leadership in establishing and expanding the Missionaries of Charity, inspiring countless others to join her cause.
  • Dedication: Her unwavering dedication to her mission and her relentless work ethic were key to her success in helping the poor and needy.
